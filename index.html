The Problem

Fuel Access Is Broken.

Fuel is essential infrastructure. Yet despite powering entire economies, access remains outdated and inefficient for millions.

01

Customers Still Travel to Stations

In a world of on-demand everything, fuel forces customers to come to it.

02

Businesses Lose Productive Time

Construction sites and logistics operations halt because of fuel access failures — not shortages.

03

Emergency Access Is Unreliable

When generators fail, there is no fast, dependable delivery solution for most communities.

04

Underserved Communities Lack Access

Rural areas face greater barriers — stations are sparse and supply chains are weak.

05

Generator Dependency Is Rising

Across Africa, generator reliance is growing with no reliable infrastructure to supply fuel on-demand.

Beyond The Main Road.

Fuel accessibility today is concentrated. Stations cluster around commercial corridors, leaving households, rural communities, and generator-dependent businesses without reliable access.

Fuelivery's delivery network extends reach far beyond where stations exist.

Asset-Light. Fast-Scale.

Fuelivery does not own fuel stations. No large real estate investment. Growth is achieved through partnerships, independent delivery networks, and technology automation.

A capital-efficient model that can expand rapidly without the overhead of traditional infrastructure companies.

Common Questions.

Get In Touch

What is Fuelivery?

+
Fuelivery is an on-demand fuel delivery platform connecting households, businesses, and communities to reliable fuel access through partnered fuel stations and a smart logistics network.

Does Fuelivery own fuel stations?

+
No. Fuelivery operates as a technology-enabled logistics platform that partners with existing fuel stations, allowing faster scaling while reducing infrastructure costs.

Is fuel delivery legal in South Africa?

+
Yes. Fuelivery is committed to full compliance with South African fuel transportation, safety, and regulatory requirements.

What stage is Fuelivery currently in?

+
Fuelivery is focused on building strategic partnerships, validating operational systems, and preparing for scalable rollout across South Africa and beyond.

Is Fuelivery seeking investors?

+
Yes. Fuelivery actively seeks strategic investors and partners aligned with our long-term vision of modernising fuel accessibility across Africa.

What markets does Fuelivery plan to expand into?

+
Initial focus is South Africa, with long-term plans to expand into major African markets and beyond.

How can businesses partner with Fuelivery?

+
Through commercial fuel supply agreements, scheduled delivery services, fleet support partnerships, and white-label logistics solutions.
